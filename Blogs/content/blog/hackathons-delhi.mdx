## Why Delhi is the Hackathon Hub of India

### The Startup Ecosystem

Delhi has the highest concentration of startups in India outside Bangalore. Companies like Swiggy, OYO, Razorpay, Lenskart, and hundreds of others have roots here. These companies often sponsor hackathons or send recruiters to scout talent. For participants, this means access to industry experts, networking, and job opportunities.

### Top Universities

- **IIT Delhi**: The flagship campus runs 2–3 major hackathons every year, often attracting students from across India.
- **Delhi University**: 15+ colleges (DU North, South, East campuses) have active CS departments and tech clubs that host regular hackathons.
- **IIIT Delhi**: Focuses on IT and CS, hosts specialised hackathons (cybersecurity, data science).

### Large Developer Communities

- **Delhi Web3 Community**: 5,000+ members interested in blockchain hackathons.
- **Delhi JS Meetup**: Regular web dev events.
- **ACM Delhi Chapter**: Coding competitions and hackathons.
- **IIT Delhi Alumni Association**: Hosts events featuring industry veterans.

### High Frequency

Hackathons in Delhi happen almost weekly during peak season (Sep–Nov, Jan–Mar). You're never more than 1–2 weeks away from a new event.

## FAQs: Hackathons in Delhi

**Q1: Where should I start if I want to find hackathons in Delhi for the first time?**

Go to FairArena.com, filter by city = "Delhi" and "Upcoming". Browse 3–5 events, read problem statements, and pick one that excites you.

**Q2: Can I participate in a Delhi hackathon if I'm not from Delhi?**

Absolutely. Most hackathons are open to anyone. Some might prioritise Delhi/NCR participants, but many welcome national participation, especially if in-person venue is needed. Always check eligibility.

**Q3: Is there a beginner-friendly hackathon in Delhi for someone coding less than 1 year?**

Yes. Look for events tagged "beginner-friendly", "junior hackathon", or run by college tech clubs. These are explicitly designed for first-timers. Avoid "advanced" or "expert-only" events until you have 6–12 months coding experience.

**Q4: How much does it cost to participate in hackathons in Delhi?**

Most are free. Some charge ₹100–₹500 registration fee. The best are 100% free and offer prizes (₹50,000–₹10,00,000+). Never pay upfront; if a hackathon asks for money before revealing prizes/details, it's likely a scam.

**Q5: How long are typical hackathons in Delhi?**

Most are 24–48 hours (continuous or split overnight). Some corporate hackathons are 12 hours (daytime). A few are asynchronous (1–4 weeks). Check the specific event details on FairArena.
