# Top Judging Software Alternatives: How to Pick the Right Evaluation Tool

You have a competition, grant program, or award event. You need judges. And you need a **fair, transparent way to evaluate submissions**.

Here's what the judging software landscape looks like—and how to choose.

## Major Players & How They Compare

### Premium Platforms (Built for Enterprise)

**Submittable** ($$$)
- Focus: Grant management, contest management, magazine submissions
- Strengths: Mature, handles complex workflows, strong security
- Weaknesses: Expensive, overkill for smaller competitions, steep learning curve
- Pricing: $300–$2,000+/month
- Best for: Large grant programs, government agencies, established award competitions

**OpenWater** ($$$)
- Focus: Grant and awards management
- Strengths: Designed specifically for funding decisions, has AI features
- Weaknesses: Very enterprise-focused, lowest price tier still expensive
- Pricing: $5,000+/year minimum
- Best for: Foundations, government grants, large corporate awards

**Reviewr** ($$)
- Focus: Blind peer review, academic research evaluation
- Strengths: Built for academic rigor, strong blind review capabilities
- Weaknesses: Niche product, academic focus, limited hackathon features
- Pricing: $50–$500/month
- Best for: Research proposals, academic competitions, peer review

### Mid-Market Options (Balanced Price & Features)

**FairArena** ($$)
- Focus: Hackathons, competitions, innovation challenges, grants
- Strengths: Customizable rubrics, blind review, multi-round judging, real-time dashboards, live event support
- Weaknesses: None significant; newer player but strong feature set
- Pricing: $200–$2,000 per event (usage-based, no subscription lock-in)
- Best for: Most hackathons, academic competitions, corporate innovation challenges

**HackerEarth** ($)
- Focus: Coding competitions and hackathons
- Strengths: Designed for developers, code testing, leaderboards, pricing scales well
- Weaknesses: Primarily for coding events, limited to technical competitions
- Pricing: $500–$5,000/event depending on scale
- Best for: Coding-focused hackathons, technical competitions

**Ideate** ($)
- Focus: Innovation challenges and internal idea management
- Strengths: Community voting, lightweight, easy to get adoption
- Weaknesses: Less structured judging, lower-quality rubric support
- Pricing: $200–$1,500/month
- Best for: Internal ideation, crowdsourced innovation

### No-Code/Low-Cost Alternatives

**Google Forms + Sheets** (Free)
- Strengths: Free, familiar, everyone has Gmail
- Weaknesses: No rubrics, no judge assignment, no automation, terrible experience at scale
- Pricing: Free
- Best for: One-off 20-person events only

**Typeform + Airtable** (Low cost)
- Strengths: Nice forms, visual database, lower learning curve
- Weaknesses: No evaluation workflows, manual reconciliation required, poor for judging
- Pricing: $25–$50/month
- Best for: Simple intake; not for structured judging

## Quick Decision Tree

**Are you running a:**

1. **Small hackathon (< 100 projects)?**
   - → FairArena (simple, fair judging) or HackerEarth (if coding-focused)

2. **Large hackathon (100-1000 projects)?**
   - → FairArena or Submittable (need complex workflows)

3. **Grant program?**
   - → Submittable or OpenWater (built for this)

4. **Academic peer review?**
   - → Reviewr (built for research rigor)

5. **Internal innovation challenge?**
   - → FairArena or Ideate (quick launch, engaging)

6. **Award competition with judges?**
   - → FairArena (fair, transparent) or Submittable (if big budget)

7. **One-time event, tiny budget?**
   - → DIY with Google Forms + Sheets (only if < 50 submissions)

## Detailed Use Case Breakdown

### **University Hackathon (300 students, 60 projects)**

Best choice: **FairArena**
- Why: Hackathon-specific features, blind review to prevent favoritism, customizable rubrics for academic evaluation
- Cost: ~$500 for one event
- Alternative: Submittable ($500–$1,000, overkill for size)

### **Corporate Innovation Challenge**

Best choice: **FairArena or Ideate**
- Why: Community voting (gets employees engaged), transparent rubrics (prevents politics), easy to repeat annually
- Cost: $300–$1,000 depending on scale
- Alternative: DIY (but you'll waste 30+ hours on admin)

### **Government Grant Program**

Best choice: **OpenWater or Submittable**
- Why: Enterprise security, compliance audit trails, no corner-cutting on governance
- Cost: $2,000–$10,000+/year
- Alternative: FairArena (works for smaller grants, lower cost)

### **Academic Peer Review (Research Papers)**

Best choice: **Reviewr**
- Why: Built specifically for research rigor, strong blind review, academic community features
- Cost: $100–$500/month
- Alternative: SubmittableOpenWater (generic but work)

### **Coding Competition**

Best choice: **HackerEarth**
- Why: Integrated code execution, leaderboards, technical community
- Cost: $500–$5,000 depending on scale
- Alternative: FairArena (fine for judging, but less technical integration)

### **Startup Pitch Competition**

Best choice: **FairArena or HackerEarth**
- Why: Multi-round judging (initial screening, finalist round, live pitches), investor dashboards
- Cost: $500–$2,000
- Alternative: Submittable (works but not optimized for live pitches)

## Red Flags: When to Avoid Certain Tools

**Don't use OpenWater if:**
- You have a budget < $5,000/year ("Definitely can't accommodate you" is their message)
- You need quick setup; they require significant consulting

**Don't use Submittable if:**
- You want low cost ($300/month minimum is real)
- You need a simple event; their complexity is overhead

**Don't use HackerEarth if:**
- You're running a non-coding competition
- You care more about fair judging than leaderboards

**Don't use Reviewr if:**
- You need community voting or engagement features
- You're outside academia

**Don't use DIY if:**
- You have more than 50 submissions
- You need structured judging (rubrics, blind review, multi-round)
- You care about time (will waste 20–40 hours on admin)

## The ROI Question: Custom vs. Off-the-Shelf

**Building something custom** (using Zapier, Airtable, Google Sheets)
- Initial time: 20–40 hours to build
- Per-event time: 5–10 hours to manage
- Cost: $0 tool cost + your/team's salaries
- Risk: Limited functionality, fragile workflows, no audit trails
- **Total cost for year (5 events**: $1,000–$2,000 in labor

**Using an off-the-shelf platform**
- Initial time: 2–4 hours to setup
- Per-event time: 1–2 hours to manage
- Cost: $300–$1,000 per event
- Benefit: Built-in functionality, audit trails, support
- **Total cost for year (5 events)**: $1,500–$5,000

**For most organizations, the platform pays for itself in organizer time.**

## Conclusion

The **best judging software** depends on your specific use case. But here's the pattern:

- **For hackathons**: FairArena or HackerEarth
- **For grants**: Submittable or OpenWater
- **For academic**: Reviewr
- **For internal innovation**: FairArena or Ideate
- **For one-time events under $500 budget**: DIY with Google Forms

The common thread? **Specialization matters.** A platform built specifically for your use case will be cheaper, faster, and produce better outcomes than forcing a generic tool to fit.
