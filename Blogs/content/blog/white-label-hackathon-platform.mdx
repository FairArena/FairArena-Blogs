# White Label Hackathon Platform: Running Competitions Under Your Brand

You want to run hackathons or competitions, but you don't want participants to see "Powered by [Generic Hackathon Platform]" at the bottom of every page.

**You want your brand front and center.**

This is where **white-label hackathon platforms** come in.

## What Is White Label?

White label means: we provide the technology; you provide the brand.

**Typical white-label hackathon platform:**
- Custom domain (competitions.yourcompany.com)
- Your logo, colors, fonts throughout
- Your terms of service, privacy policy
- No mention of the underlying platform
- Participants think: "This is [YourCompany]'s custom platform"

**What you don't do:**
- Build or maintain the infrastructure
- Handle server security or scaling
- Manage integrations, APIs, database design
- Handle customer support chat bots

**You focus on:**
- Running great competitions
- Marketing to participants
- Judging and selecting winners
- Building your community

## Why Organizations Need White Label

### **Case 1: Corporate Hackathons**
Company X runs annual employee hackathons. They want the portal to feel like part of their internal tools, not a public platform. White label ensures:
- Branded domain (ideas.company.com)
- Internal-only styling
- Custom data retention (all ideas stay in our systems, not platform's)
- Feel like proprietary internal tool

### **Case 2: University Hackathons**
Universities run student hackathons and want:
- Branded portal (hack.university.edu)
- Integration with campus authentication (Okta/SAML)
- Student feel the same university experience
- Participants remember "University Hackathon" not the platform

### **Case 3: Series of Events**
Event organizer runs 10 hackathons per year across different regions/verticals. White label enables:
- Consistent branding across all events
- Unified admin dashboard
- Participants experience your brand, not 10 different platforms
- Single point of control

### **Case 4: Government/NGO**
Civic hackathon organizers want:
- Custom domain (innovate.govt.agency)
- Trustworthy look (government branding)
- Local customization

- Backend infrastructure

## White Label vs. Self-Hosted vs. DIY

### **Platform with White Label** (FairArena, etc.)
- Pros: Branded, no infrastructure burden, scales automatically
- Cons: Less control over code, can't modify core features
- Cost: $1,000–$10,000/year
- Time to launch: 2-4 weeks
- Best for: Most organizations that want branding without tech overhead

### **Self-Hosted Open Source**
- Pros: Full control, no recurring fees, always available
- Cons: Requires engineering team, security/maintenance burden, scaling complexity
- Cost: $30,000–$100,000/year in engineering labor
- Time to launch: 6-12 months
- Best for: Organizations with elite engineering teams and control requirements

### **Build From Scratch**
- Pros: 100% custom, exactly what you want
- Cons: 12-24 month build, $100,000–$500,000+ cost, ongoing maintenance
- Cost: $200,000–$500,000+ first year, $50,000+/year ongoing
- Time to launch: 12-24 months
- Best for: Almost nobody (too expensive, too slow)

### **Spreadsheets + Email** (DIY terrible)
- Pros: Free initial cost
- Cons: Doesn't scale past 30 projects, terrible experience, errors everywhere
- Cost: Your time (40-80 hours per event) + salary
- Time to launch: 1 week (but quality is terrible)
- Best for: One-off micro-events only

## Choosing a White Label Hackathon Platform

**Key questions:**

1. **Branding customization**:
   - Can I use a custom domain?
   - Can I fully customize colors/logo/fonts?
   - Or are there limitations?

2. **Feature parity**:
   - Does white label include all features (rubric judging, blind review, etc.)?
   - Or is white label a limited version?

3. **Authentication**:
   - Can I integrate with my authentication (Active Directory, Okta)?
   - Or does it use platform's auth only?

4. **Data ownership**:
   - Do I own all participant data?
   - Can I export everything?
   - Who's liable for data breaches?

5. **Support**:
   - Dedicated account manager?
   - Priority support?
   - Response time?

6. **Pricing transparency**:
   - What's included in white label vs. add-ons?
   - Overage costs?
   - Can I lock in pricing year-over-year?

7. **Migration**:
   - Can I export all data if I switch platforms?
   - Is migration supported?

8. **SLA & uptime**:
   - Is there an uptime SLA (99.9%, 99.99%)?
   - What happens if system goes down during event?

## Common Pitfalls

**Pitfall 1: Underestimating branding complexity**
- Don't just slap on a logo
- Think about every page, email, notification
- Consistency matters

**Pitfall 2: Forgetting legal customization**
- Your T&Cs and privacy policy should be front & center
- Don't hide behind "See platform's privacy policy"
- Gets you legally responsible (which you should be)

**Pitfall 3: Lock-in culture**
- If platform shuts down or raises prices dramatically, you're stuck
- Always negotiate data portability in contract

**Pitfall 4: Limited customization**
- Ask upfront what can be customized
- Some platforms have rigid limits
- Ensure it meets your branding requirements

## Conclusion

**White label is the sweet spot** for most organizations that want to run competitions professionally:
- Branded experience (your domain, your logo, your look)
- Zero infrastructure burden
- Scales automatically

- You need 100% custom technology (build from scratch)

- You're running 2+ events/year
- Branding and control matter to you
- You want a professional experience for participants
- You don't want infrastructure headaches
- Cost is a concern
