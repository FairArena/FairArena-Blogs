# How to Run a Successful Virtual Judging Panel

You've got 10 finalists. Now it's go-time: live online judging where judges evaluate projects in real-time, audience watches, and winners are crowned.

This is high-stakes event management. Here's the playbook.

## Pre-Event (2 Weeks Before)

**Confirm judges:**
- Email: "You'll be part of live judging panel. 2-hour Zoom. Video on. Are you available?"
- Get confirmation + test tech (Zoom, video/audio work)
- Provide: Finalists' projects + rubric + your criteria

**Prepare projects:**
- Each finalist gets 8-10 minute presentation time
- Finalists demo on stream OR organizer screen-shares their work
- Judges ask questions (3-4 questions per project)

**Create format:**
```
6:00 PM - Welcome + judge introductions (5 min)
6:05 PM - Project 1: Presentation (8 min) + Q&A (7 min)
6:20 PM - Project 2: Presentation (8 min) + Q&A (7 min)
...
7:40 PM - Judging deliberation (15 min, judges + organizers off-camera)
7:55 PM - Winners announced (5 min)
```

## Technical Setup (1 Week Before)

**Zoom setup:**
- Separate breakout rooms: Main stage, Judge deliberation
- Recording enabled (attendees may ask for proof of judging)
- Screenshare demos tested
- Backup WiFi link (judge internet fails? Dial-in by phone)

**Production quality:**
- Good lighting (judges visible on camera)
- High-quality audio (lapel mics or good USB mics)
- Virtual background (branded or neutral)
- Slides for titles/names ("Judge: Sarah Chen, CEO of X")

**Smooth project demos:**
- Test every finalist's demo (GitHub link works? Figma prototype loads? Video plays?)
- Have backup: If demo fails, play pre-recorded video instead
- Org team ready to troubleshoot (1 person monitoring tech,1 person running Q&A)

## Judge Briefing (1 Day Before)

**30-minute call with judges:**
- Walk through format (who speaks when, how Q&A works)
- Explain rubric (what you're scoring on)
- Discuss judge diversity (can you ask about market? Team? Product?)
- Set expectations: "You'll deliberate together for 15 min; we announce winners live"

**Provide judges:**
- List of projects + descriptions
- Judge credentials (who they are, why they matter)
- Rubric printed/digital
- Notes on each finalist's background

## Event Day

### **Pre-Stream (30 min before)**
- Judges join early; tech tested
- Projects confirmed ready to demo
- Run through opening remarks
- Sound checks done

### **Live Stream Opening (5 min)**
- Organizer: "Welcome! Here's what you're about to see..."
- Judge introductions: "Meet our incredible panel..."
- Stakes: "We're awarding $[X] to winners; selecting based on [criteria]"

### **Project Presentations (8 min each)**

**Format per project:**
1. **Introduction (org)**: "Project 1: AI Therapist. Team: John, Sarah, Mike" (1 min)
2. **Presentation (finalist OR screenshare)**: Demo + pitch (7 min)
3. **Questions (judges)**: Each judge asks 1-2 questions (5-7 min)
4. **Judge scores recorded silently** (judges fill out rubric as they listen)

**Moderator's job:**
- Keep time (bell at 8-min mark)
- Signal "One more question" at 6-min mark
- Repeat names for record
- Keep energy high ("Brilliant idea, team!")

### **Judge Deliberation (15 min, OFF-CAMERA)**
- Move judges to breakout room
- They discuss: "Who stood out? Who was close? Who should win?"
- Organizer: "You have 14 minutes. Report back."
- Organizer tallies actual scores if available

### **Winner Announcement (5 min)**
- Organizer returns from breakout room
- "After careful deliberation... 1st place goes to..."
- Judges applaud
- Winners appear on camera (if available)
- Brief winner remarks (30 seconds each)

## During-Stream Communication

**Chat moderation:**
- Keep chat positive (disable negativity)
- Answer questions: "How long are judges deliberating?" "When are results?"
- Manage expectations: "We'll announce winner in ~20 minutes"

**Energy management:**
- Use emojis, reactions to celebrate
- Call out great questions judges asked
- Build drama: "This is incredibly close..."

## Post-Stream

**Immediately:**
- Thank judges + finalists publicly
- Share judging scores (optional but builds trust)
- Share judge feedback (if appropriate)

**Within 24 hours:**
- Send winners their prize details
- Send non-winners feedback from judges
- Share recording with attendees
- Post highlights on social media

## Common Issues & Fixes

**Judge goes off-camera during deliberation**
- **Fix**: Remind judges before: "Video on during deliberation, mics muted"

**Tech fails during live demo**
- **Fix**: Have pre-recorded backup video ready
- Org team: "We're experiencing technical issues. Here's the recorded demo"

**Judge asks harsh/inappropriate question**
- **Fix**: Train judges upfront on appropriate Q&A
- Moderator: Gently redirect ("That's interesting... Let me rephrase that question for clarity")

**Judge scores wildly different from others**
- **Fix**: This is fine; different perspectives are valuable
- Mention during deliberation: "Judge A sees X differently; let's discuss"

**Finalists don't show up**
- **Fix**: Confirm attendance 24 hours before with reminder email
- Have backup finalist ready to present (next-highest seed)

## Judging Panel Success Checklist

**1 week before:**
- [ ] Judges confirmed + tech tested
- [ ] Projects demos tested
- [ ] Rubric shared with judges
- [ ] Format walkthrough scheduled

**1 day before:**
- [ ] Judge briefing call completed
- [ ] Tech full run-through done
- [ ] Finalists sent presentation tips + backup demo

**Day of:**
- [ ] Judges online 30 min early
- [ ] Audio/video quality checked
- [ ] Chat moderation plan in place
- [ ] Timekeeper ready (keep project segments to schedule)

**During stream:**
- [ ] Moderator keeps time
- [ ] Questions stay constructive
- [ ] Judges deliberate off-camera
- [ ] Winner announce is clear + celebratory

## Conclusion

A polished virtual judging event:
- Builds credibility ("Look how fair our process is")
- Engages community (public voting confidence)
- Celebrates winners (creates FOMO for next year)
- Showcases finalists (great PR for 10 teams)

It's 2-3 hours of live content that sets the tone for your entire competition.

**Before your next judging event**: Do a full tech dry-run with judges. Time-test the format. Fix issues before live. Your audience will notice professionalism.
